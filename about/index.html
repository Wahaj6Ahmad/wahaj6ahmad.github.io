About

I grew up in a family of tinkerers who believed they could fix anything. There’s a running joke that when we call a technician, they get paid to hold the torch while we do the work. When I was eleven, I replaced the blown battery in my sister’s toy laptop, and it flipped a switch for me. I wanted to understand how things work, and I wanted to build them.

Today, I’m a robotics and mechatronics systems engineer with a background in interaction technology. I work at the intersection of hardware, software, and people. I’ve done embedded and electromechanical integration, field installation and troubleshooting, and research in human–robot interaction. I like making systems stable, testable, and usable.

I’m looking for teams where I can contribute quickly, keep learning, and build products that matter. I’m interested in AI too, but only when it adds real value, not as a marketing checkbox.

Outside work, I like chess, the gym, squash, games, and DIY projects.
